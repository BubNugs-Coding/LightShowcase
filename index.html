LIGHT

Season One: Awakening

Light is a high-fantasy world where select individuals are born with elemental and cosmic powers that shape the realms they inhabit. This project focuses on developing a long-form serialized narrative following Lucas Alderaunt and the realm of Serenia. Season One establishes the emotional and structural foundation of the larger twelve-season arc.

Read the Season One story

The Story

Season One introduces Lucas Alderaunt, an ordinary boy in Serenia who has always admired the realm's legendary power wielders but believes he is destined for a mundane life. That belief is shattered when he discovers he is the new Warrior of Light, a title once carried by heroes who shaped Serenia's history. The kingdom itself was founded by a former Warrior of Light, placing immense legacy and expectation on Lucas from the moment he awakens.

As Lucas struggles to understand his power and what it means to truly be "chosen," he forms alliances with the Warriors of Fire, Air, Water, and Earth. At the same time, he faces Derrik, the Warrior of Darkness, who seeks the ancient Realm Crystal for his own ends. The Realm Crystal allows passage between Serenia and the neighboring Realm of the Dead, a boundary that must remain stable to preserve the natural order.

A parallel conflict unfolds when one of Lucas's allies is revealed to be an escaped ghost, drawing the attention of the Realmkeeper, guardian of the Realm of the Dead. To prevent further destabilization, Lucas negotiates a fragile peace: he will retrieve the stolen Realm Crystal before its misuse tears open the boundary between realms.

In the finale, Derrik activates the Crystal in desperation, creating an unstable tear between life and death. The tear begins to unravel reality itself, and Aira, the Warrior of Air, sacrifices herself to enter the rupture and stabilize it from within. Both she and the Crystal vanish. Believing her dead, Lucas confronts Derrik in rage, nearly becoming what he hates. In a final moment of vulnerability, Derrik reveals the weight of his choices before throwing himself from the cliff.

Season One ends not with triumph, but with consequence. Lucas learns that power alone does not make someone worthy—restraint and responsibility do. The emotional fallout of these events sets the stage for the larger arc of the series.

The Realm of Serenia

Serenia is the central realm of the story, a kingdom shaped by previous Warriors of Light. It sits unusually close to another realm — the Realm of the Dead — creating instability in the natural flow of souls.

Home of the current king of Serenia and the royal mages. Explored more in seasons two and three.

High up in the peaks of Warrior's Mountain, Derrik accidentally tore a hole in the realms. Aira was able to close it at the cost of her life and the realm crystal. Also the site of Derrik's supposed death.

Derrik's home, once part of Serenia itself before it was torn away by an ancient Warrior of Earth.

Season One — Structural Breakdown

Season One is structured around escalating boundary instability, mirroring Lucas's growing loss of emotional control.

Episode 1 — Awakening

  • Lucas discovers he is the Warrior of Light.
  • First confrontation with Derrik.
  • The Realm Crystal is introduced.

Episode 2 — Fractures

  • Tension grows within the team.
  • The escaped ghost subplot begins to surface.
  • Lucas struggles with leadership and expectation.

Episode 3 — Boundaries

  • The Realmkeeper confronts the crew.
  • Lucas negotiates a fragile agreement.
  • Derrik's pressure and desperation become more visible.

Episode 4 — Theft

  • Derrik steals the Realm Crystal.
  • Instability begins to form at the boundary.
  • Emotional fractures intensify.

Episode 5 — Collapse

  • The Crystal is activated recklessly.
  • A destabilizing tear forms.
  • Reality begins to strain under misuse.

Episode 6 — The Fall

  • Aira enters the rupture to stabilize it.
  • Both Aira and the Crystal vanish.
  • Lucas confronts Derrik in rage.
  • Derrik says, "Better you than him," before throwing himself from the cliff.

The Structure of Life and Death

Souls normally travel to The End.

Serenia's proximity to the Realm of the Dead causes misdirection.

Ghosts are displaced souls, not undead.

The Realm Crystal allows controlled passage between Serenia and the Realm of the Dead.

Misuse destabilizes the boundary.
